# Product

## Register

product

## Users

French-speaking parents and families renting baby/child equipment (strollers, car seats, cribs, toys) for travel, vacations, or short-term use. They use the app on mobile while planning a trip or on-the-go. Primary task on any screen: browse → reserve → pay → track their rental.

## Product Purpose

Kits & Kids is a baby equipment rental marketplace. Users rent clean, insured equipment delivered to their door, hotel, or airport. Success = a seamless booking flow from discovery to confirmed reservation, with full confidence the equipment is safe and hygienically maintained.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, warm, effortless. The brand should feel like a reliable friend who happens to have great baby gear — professional but approachable, never clinical or cold. Safety and cleanliness are the core promise.

## Anti-references

- Avoid sterile medical / clinical white UIs (cold, impersonal)
- Avoid overdesigned luxury / fashion-app aesthetics (mismatched to parents on the go)
- Avoid busy marketplace clutter (Amazon-style information overload)
- Generic SaaS dashboard patterns (navy-and-gold fintech, gray admin panels)

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence at every step** — users are trusting us with their child's safety; every screen must radiate reliability through consistent, polished UI.
2. **Flow over features** — the booking funnel is sacred. Friction, confusion, or cropped buttons anywhere in the funnel is a critical bug.
3. **Identity over genericness** — the Kits & Kids brand (navy, light blue, the K. stroller logo) should be recognizable and consistent across every screen.
4. **Clarity before cleverness** — labels, CTAs, and statuses should be unambiguous in French. When in doubt, be direct.
5. **Mobile-first, thumb-friendly** — all interactive targets ≥ 48px, primary actions reachable from the bottom of the screen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. French-language UI throughout. Sufficient contrast on all text (≥4.5:1 body, ≥3:1 large text). Touch targets ≥ 48×48dp. Reduced motion should be respected for splash/onboarding animations.
